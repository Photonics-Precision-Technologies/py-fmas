1.2. fmas.stepper module¶

The \(z\)-propagation schemes implemented with the pyFMAS package are specified by a \(z\)-propagation algorithm along with a \(z\)-stepping formula that performs the field update for a single step.

Currently, pyFMAS implements a second-order Runge-Kutta and a fourth-order Runge-Kutta formula for \(z\)-stepping.

fmas.stepper.RungeKutta2(fun, z, uw, dz)¶

Second-order Runge-Kutta formula

Implements second-order Runge-Kutta formula for \(z\)-stepping [1]. Achieves local error \(\mathcal O(\Delta z^3)\), with step size \(\Delta z\).

References

[1] W. H. Press, S. A. Teukolsky, W. T. Vetterling, B. P. Flannery, Numerical Recipes in C: The art of scientific computing (Chapter 16.1), Cambridge University Press (1992).

Parameters

  • fun (function) – Function evaluating the evolution rate of the ODE.

  • z (float) – Current \(z\)-value.

  • uw (numpy.ndarray, 1-dim) – Frequency-domain representation of the current field.

  • dz (float) – Step size.

fmas.stepper.RungeKutta4(fun, z, uw, dz)¶

Fourth-order Runge-Kutta formula

Implements fourth-order Runge-Kutta formula for \(z\)-stepping [1]. Achieves local error \(\mathcal O(\Delta z^5)\), with step size \(\Delta z\).

References

[1] W. H. Press, S. A. Teukolsky, W. T. Vetterling, B. P. Flannery, Numerical Recipes in C: The art of scientific computing (Chapter 16.1), Cambridge University Press (1992).

Parameters

  • fun (function) – Function evaluating the evolution rate of the ODE.

  • z (float) – Current \(z\)-value.

  • uw (numpy.ndarray, 1-dim) – Frequency-domain representation of the current field.

  • dz (float) – Step size.
